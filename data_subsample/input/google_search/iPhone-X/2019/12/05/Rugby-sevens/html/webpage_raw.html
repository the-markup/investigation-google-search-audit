    Description

    Rugby sevens, and originally known as seven-a-side rugby, is a variant of rugby union in which teams are made up of seven players playing seven minute halves, instead of the usual 15 players playing 40 minute halves. Rugby sevens is administered by World Rugby, the body responsible for rugby union worldwide. Wikipedia
    Highest governing body: World Rugby
    World Games: 2001 – 2013
    First played: 1883
    Team members: 7
    Nicknames: Sevens, 7s, VIIs, Seven-a-side
    Olympic: 2016 onwards
