      Description

      A loss of normal function of the ovaries before age 40.
      In early menopause, the ovaries don't produce normal amounts of the hormone estrogen or release eggs regularly. Infertility is common. However, it's possible a woman with this condition could get pregnant with treatment.
      Rare
      Fewer than 200,000 US cases per year
      Treatment can help, but this condition can't be cured
      Requires a medical diagnosis
      Lab tests or imaging always required
      Chronic: can last for years or be lifelong
      Consult a doctor for medical advice
