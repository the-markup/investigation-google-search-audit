    About

    Description

    Navajo Generating Station was a 2.25-gigawatt, coal-fired power plant located on the Navajo Nation, near Page, Arizona United States. This plant provided electrical power to customers in Arizona, Nevada, and California. Wikipedia
    Closed: November 18, 2019
    Owners: United States Bureau of Reclamation (24.3%), Salt River Project (21.7%), More
    Units operational: 3 × 803 MW
    Annual net output: 12,059 GW·h (2016)
    Opened: 1976
    Construction cost: 650 million USD (1976, $2.19 billion in 2016 dollars)
    Questions & answers
    Q: Does this power plant have any Air Quality Control Devices, e.g., Scrubbers, Bag Houses, SCRs, Precipatators or any other Clean Coal Technology Devices?
    A: Yes. It has SCR's, bag houses on the fly ash silos it also has mercury removal in the stacks. And it does have preciptators that remove fly ash into ash hoppers.
